Overview

In general, the buildings in which we live are thermally inefficient, causing to most of the energy we use to heat them to escape outside, which provides little benefit to us. Since domestic energy consumption accounts for approximately 24% of UK energy demand and, also for the largest peaks in demand, it is crucial that we all reduce how much energy we use in our homes. One study has shown that we need to reduce domestic demand by 57% if we are to transision to a decarbonised energy system and stay inside 2C of global warming.

The aim of the CHEESE project is to reduce domestic energy consumption, primarily from heat losses. We do this by performing detailed surveys of buildings using low-cost thermal imaging equipment to identify areas where simple but effective remedial action can be taken. It is estimated that 30% of heating losses are due to drafts, which can be fixed cheaply and easily. By recording 'before and after' energy consumption, the impact of our surveys can be verified.

Funding

The CHEESE pilot project was setup in March 2015 with funding from Bristol Green Capital 2015. Two initial small grants of £5,000 were awarded to Easton Energy Group and Ambition Lawrence Weston . A further two grants were awarded to the Bishopston, Cotham and Redland Neighbourhood Partnership of £2,000 and £1,610.

Our partner organisations:
  • Centre for Sustainable Energy
  • Bristol Energy Network
  • Ambition Lawrence Weston
  • Easton Energy Group
  • Bishopston, Cotham, Redland Energy Group
  • Transition Malvern Hills

The CHEESE project is a not-for-profit community enterprise that aims to reduce energy bills, improve health and wellbeing, and reduce our carbon emissions.
